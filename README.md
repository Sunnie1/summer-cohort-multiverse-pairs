# Summer Cohort · Multiverse Pairs

This is the cohort's internal documentation. It is the record of who we are and what we are all building.

You are about to open your first pull request against a repo you don't own. That's the skill today.

---

## The challenge

You got paired with somebody. Go find them, talk to them, then write them down here.

**15 minutes. All of it:**

1. Find your partner in the cohort chat. Don't wait for me.
2. Talk. Ask what they're building and who it's for.
3. Write them up in this repo and commit it.
4. Come back and tell the class **one thing you learned about their project.** Not yours.

---

## How to add your partner

All in the browser. No terminal needed.

1. Click into the **`people/`** folder above.
2. **Add file** → **Create new file**.
3. Name it `people/theirhandle.md` (your **partner's** handle, not yours).
4. Paste the template below and fill it in.
5. Scroll down. Your button says **Propose changes**. Click it, then **Create pull request**.

### Wait, why does it say "propose changes" and not "commit"?

Because you don't have write access to this repo, and you shouldn't. Nobody hands push access to a stranger.

So GitHub does something helpful: it makes you your own copy of the repo (a **fork**), puts your file there, and offers to send it to me as a **pull request**. That's you asking me to pull your work into my project.

This is how essentially every open source contribution on earth works. Keep clicking, the words are new but the buttons are right. Nico merges them live, and your name lands in the repo.

## The template

```markdown
# their-handle

**What they're building:**
One or two sentences. Plain words.

**Who it's for:**
Be specific. "Solo therapists" beats "small businesses."

**One thing I'd want to know next:**
The question you'd ask if you had another five minutes.

**Where to find them:**
Their site, their GitHub, their handle. Whatever they gave you.

*Written by: [your handle]*
```

See `people/EXAMPLE.md` for what a filled-in one looks like.

## Rules

- **Your words.** Not a paste from Claude. The whole point is that you listened to a human. Claude wasn't in that conversation.
- **One file per person**, named after your **partner**.
- **Name it after them, not you.** If two people write about the same person, we'll sort it out.
- **If it's rough, commit it anyway.** Rough and shipped beats polished and never.

## If your partner isn't here

Say so in the cohort chat and grab the next person who says the same thing. If you're still on your own, DM anyone on the list.

That's not a workaround. That's outreach. You're doing the thing.

## Why this repo exists

Dunbar's number: you can hold about 150 relationships in your head, and even that is generous. 5 close, 15, 50, 150. Past that, your head leaks.

This cohort fits inside that number, which means we can actually know each other. But your head is not a database, and a group that wants to remember anything has to write it down. That's what internal documentation is: what a team keeps for itself.

This is yours. Keep it current.
